A Brief Guide to a Better Email: How to write support emails that your customers will love

Answering truckloads of emails all day can wear down one’s ability to maintain top notch customer service. From the start of the work day to the very end, it’s important to make sure every email comes off as professional, friendly, and helpful all at the same time. This article from HelpScout.net shows the good the bad and the ugly of customer service emails. It suggests which phrases to stay away from, and which ones to make sure you use.

Each person we email only has a select few points of contact in our company, even though you may have dozens in theirs. Build rapport! Make them feel comfortable coming to you with questions or concerns! We are here to help and we want to make sure using and selling our product is as simple and easy as possible so they come back time and time again. Read this article for some helpful tips on how to maintain great customer service in our emails!

15 Tips for Successfully Handling Customer Complaints

The chances are good you have already or may receive a customer complaint, either from a sales representative or an un-satisfied client. Although a complaint like this can cause frustration, have you ever looked at criticism from a client as an opportunity to create and maintain a positive relationship towards the future? Well, according to Gregory Ciotti, your outlook should be just that!

“I genuinely believe that any business can create a competitive advantage through giving outstanding customer care.”
– Gary Vaynerchuk

Although handling complaints correctly takes practice and persistence, the opportunity that can come from satisfying a disgruntled client can yield a large reward in the future. Throughout the entirety of Ciotti’s article, he dives in to strategies for handling customer complaints. He describes how handling a complaint incorrectly can “hurt the bottom line.” With this being said, the featured article focuses on 15 well thought out tips, for successfully handling these kind of complaints and why they should matter to you.
